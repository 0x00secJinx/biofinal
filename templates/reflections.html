Reflections

Evolution:

Evolution is the overriding theme in biology. We see this with many aspects in biology with the idea of a common ancestor and many other cases of allelic frequency flips. Evolution is the change in allele frequency in a population which occurs over hundreds to thousands of years. An important note is that evolution does not say that humans come from monkeys, which is a main point in the debate over evolution. One example of evolution that was given during this class was the case of the sticklebacks fish. They have a trait for a spine on the bottom of their body, with the spine being the dominant trait and no spine is the recessive. In the ocean, this spine helps them survive by preventing them from being eaten, but when some migrated to freshwater lakes, the spine game them a reproductive disadvantage. This reproductive disadvantage, where a dragonfly nymph can grab their spine and eat them, caused a shift in the allele frequency where more fish in te freshwater do not have spines. This new population in the lake is not a new species, but the population evolved to fit their environment easier.

Biology Debate:

Many of the medical advances and medical technologies come from biology. If we did not have biology, many people would not have the neccessary medical treatments that help them get better and heal. From everything from biochemistry to biotech, advances are being made daily that will help improve our quality of life. These advancements help us understand the natural world around us and could help improve our environment, and teach us how to respect the natural flow of life. The study of biology also is a humbling topic as we realize that humans are not the center of life. Many creatures and organisms are important to us in our daily lifes and our evolution. For example, much of the food we eat are helped digest by E. Coli living in our guts. These organisms were here long before humans and they help us digest food. These relationships between organisms are also part of the study of biology. Symbiotic relationships are a necessity for life and they can also explain some theories of evolution. The mitochondria that make our ATP and chloroplasts that are in plants are in a symbiotic relationship with the cells. The mitochondria have their own DNA and can replicate themselves so the theory of endosymbiosis states that the mitochondria were consumed by the cells and the the cell used them to get energy for it's processes. Without all this knowledge, we could not be where we are today with the technology we have, and the knowledge of the body and the processes in it.
