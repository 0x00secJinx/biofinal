Interrelated Topics:
Cell Membrane Functions & Biochemistry

Explaination:

The first artifact shown is a membrane function pogil, where we got into groups and worked through the packet using information from the pages and communicating to try and figure out the topic and information by ourselves. After we completed the page, we got together as a class to discuss the packet. This helps us learn because we are discussing and figuring out the topic ourselves without having it told to us, which helps us retain the information. In the first packet, it goes over how differing composition of substances effect how easily the substances pass through the membrane. The second artifact is a biochemistry basics pogil. In this pogil, it goes over the stucture of the four different polymers and how their structure affects their shape and functions.

These two topics are interrelated in that the structure of the polymers affect how they pass throught the membrane. Without the basic knowledge of these substances, we could not understand how the cell membrane works and how the body functions. We also see how proteins help in passive and active transport based on the composition and structure of the materials trying to pass through. For example, glucose is a neccessity to life, but it is too large to pass through the membrane, so it needs these proteins in the membrane to get into the cell.
