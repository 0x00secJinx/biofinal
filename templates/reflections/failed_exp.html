"Failed" Experiment:
Acid Rain on Peach peroxidase

Explaination:

My most memorable failed experiment was an inquiry lab based off of the turnip peroxidase lab when we were studying enzymes. The experiment was testing the effects of acid rain on peaches as these are an important crop in my location, and acid rain is a worldwide epidemic. While doing my research for this project, I found a reliable source that said there was a large concentration of peroxidase in the skins of peaches, but as it turns out, there is little to none.

The peroxidase enzyme breaks down hydrogen peroxide, but when I poured it on the peach once we started the lab, no reaction occured. Still, we continued in hopes that is would work. We made our simulated acid rain (3 drops of sulfuric acid in 1 liter of water) and put it on the peach. Once we let it soak, we made our peroxidase "concentrated" solution and began the experiment. After 10 trials, nothing happened. No reactions of any kind occured and that is when I realized that my partner and I failed this experiment.
